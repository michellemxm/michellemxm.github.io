Kiro Agentic IDE

Overview

Kiro is AWS's agentic IDE, built around a spec-driven workflow that helps developers move from requirements to technical design to structured tasks with the support of AI agents. It goes beyond code recommendation or single-task completions through chat, enabling developers to work with agents that understand context and execute across the full development lifecycle.

My Role

As design lead of Kiro, I define how agentic capabilities come to life in the IDE, ensuring the workflows are intuitive, transparent, and productive. I drive design strategy and collaborate closely with product, engineering, and science teams to continuously evolve Kiro while aligning it with AWS's broader developer tools ecosystem.

The Challenge

Bridging the gap between product requirements and code implementation

Developers often struggle with the disconnect between what needs to be built and how it gets built. Traditional development workflows rely on scattered documentation, ad-hoc conversations, and individual interpretation, leading to misaligned features, rework, and inefficient development cycles. As AI-powered coding tools emerged, most focused on code generation in isolation, missing the broader context of why code is being written in the first place.

Kiro was created to address this fundamental gap by introducing a structured, spec-driven approach that brings clarity and intentionality to every stage of the development lifecycle, from requirements gathering through implementation.

Core Innovation

Spec-Driven Workflow

At the heart of Kiro is the spec-driven workflow, a structured methodology that guides developers through three key phases: requirements, design, and implementation tasks. Rather than jumping straight into code, Kiro encourages developers to articulate what they're building and why before determining how.

This approach ensures alignment between product intent and technical execution, reduces ambiguity, and creates a shared understanding that persists throughout the project. The spec becomes a living document that evolves with the codebase, keeping the team grounded in the original goals while adapting to new insights.

Agentic Experience

Designing for AI agents that understand context

Kiro's agentic capabilities go beyond simple code completion. The AI agents in Kiro understand the full development context, from the spec and requirements down to the codebase structure, enabling them to execute meaningful tasks across the entire development lifecycle.

Designing this experience required careful consideration of transparency, control, and trust. Developers need to understand what the agent is doing, why it's making certain decisions, and how to steer it when needed. We focused on creating interaction patterns that keep the developer in the driver's seat while letting the agent handle the heavy lifting, striking a balance between automation and agency.

Design Principles

Guiding the design of an agentic IDE

The design of Kiro is grounded in a set of core principles that shape every interaction:

  • Transparency: Make the agent's reasoning and actions visible so developers can build trust and maintain oversight
  • Progressive disclosure: Surface the right level of detail at the right time, avoiding information overload while keeping depth accessible
  • Developer agency: Ensure the developer always has control, with the ability to guide, override, or refine the agent's work
  • Seamless integration: Fit naturally into existing development workflows and mental models rather than requiring developers to learn entirely new paradigms
Impact

Launching Kiro to the developer community

Kiro launched publicly, introducing developers worldwide to a new way of building software. The spec-driven workflow and agentic capabilities resonated with developers looking for more structured, intentional approaches to AI-assisted development. The response validated our design hypothesis: developers want AI that enhances their thinking process, not just their typing speed.

As design lead, I continue to evolve Kiro's experience based on user feedback, usage patterns, and emerging capabilities, ensuring the product grows alongside the rapidly evolving landscape of AI-powered development tools.
