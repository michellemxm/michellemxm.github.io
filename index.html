Kiro Agentic IDE

AWS • 2024-2025

Agentic Experience Developer Tool

Kiro is AWS’s new agentic IDE, built around a spec-driven workflow that helps developers move from requirements to technical design to structured tasks with the support of AI agents. It goes beyond code recommendation or single-task completions through chat, enabling developers to work with agents that understand context and execute across the full development lifecycle.
As design lead of Kiro, I define how these agentic capabilities come to life in the IDE, ensuring the workflows are intuitive, transparent, and productive. I also drive design strategy and collaborate closely with product, engineering, and science teams to continuously evolve Kiro while aligning it with AWS’s broader developer tools ecosystem.

Alexa+ on Mobile App

Amazon • 2023-2024

Mobile App AI Assistant

Alexa+ is Amazon’s new generative AI–powered evolution of Alexa, designed to deliver more natural conversations, personalized assistance, and intelligent task automation across devices.
As the design lead for Alexa+ on the Alexa mobile app, I spearhead the integration of these AI-powered features into the mobile experience, ensuring they are accessible, intuitive, and impactful on-the-go. My role bridges mobile with other endpoints such as Echo and web, collaborating with fellow design leads to maintain a consistent user experience across platforms. Alongside enabling new AI-driven functionality, I continue to evolve core functional pillars with enhanced design solutions, while driving design planning and contributing to the overall product strategy.

Alexa Mosaic Design System

Mosaic is a design system created for the Alexa mobile team and the partner teams (2020–2024) to speed up design and ensure consistency. It provides tokens, components, layouts, and interaction frameworks, enabling designers and engineers to build faster with shared patterns.

Google HWUX

In summer 2018, I was a UX Design Intern on Google’s Hardware UX team in Mountain View. I envisioned new experiences for the 2019–2020 product ecosystem, identifying device value and defining 12 cross-product opportunities. I delivered detailed storyboards and designed key user interactions.

AOAB

Art on the Atlanta BeltLine is the South’s largest free outdoor art exhibition, spanning a 22-mile loop of trails and parks through 45 neighborhoods. Our project enhances this experience with a mobile site offering geolocation-based audio tours, community forums, and event tools - it connects visitors with art, artists, and the local community in an engaging way.

VISSH

Voice Interaction Surface for Smart Homes is a wearable patch that extends smart speaker reach. Designed by the COSMOS Team at Georgia Tech, it uses a self-sustained flexible microphone and attaches to clothing to enable on-body voice interactions anywhere at home.

SWAN 2.0

SWAN 2.0 is a portable navigation aid that fuses indoor positioning with 3D audio cues. It guides users through indoor spaces with precise localization, supporting both people with vision loss and first responders in low-visibility environments while enhancing autonomy and situational awareness.
